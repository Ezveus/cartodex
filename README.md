# Cartodex

This application is a card manager for Pokémon Trading Card Game.

It is available in French and English.

## Main features

### Collection manager

Keep track of which cards in how much cards you own.

Scan real cards using webcam.

### Deck manager

Keep track of which cards are used in a deck, of wins/losses by deck.

Print decklists.

Deck builder.

## Card types

Here are the main types of cards in the Pokémon Trading Card Game and their key characteristics.

### Pokémon Cards

These are the primary cards that battle each other. Their main characteristics include:
- HP (Hit Points) at the top right showing how much damage they can take
- Stage (Basic, Stage 1, or Stage 2) indicating evolution level
- Type symbol that affects attack matchups
  - Grass: Represents grass, bug, and poison Pokémon
  - Fire: Fire-type Pokémon
  - Water: Water and ice-type Pokémon
  - Lightning: Electric-type Pokémon
  - Fighting: Fighting, ground, and rock Pokémon
  - Psychic: Psychic and ghost Pokémon
  - Darkness: Dark-type Pokémon
  - Metal: Steel-type Pokémon
  - Fairy: Fairy-type Pokémon
  - Dragon: Dragon-type Pokémon
  - Colorless: Normal, flying, and some unique Pokémon
- Move/Attack costs showing required energy
- Weakness and Resistance to specific types
- Retreat Cost indicating energy needed to switch out
- Pokédex information and collector details at the bottom
  - Set Symbol: Located in bottom right, indicates which set the card is from
  - Card Number: Shows the card's number within the set (e.g., 25/165)
  - Rarity Symbol: Located next to set symbol
    - Circle: Common
    - Diamond: Uncommon
    - Star: Rare
    - Star H: Holo Rare
    - Ultra Rare variants: Full Art, Secret Rare, Rainbow Rare, etc.
  - Copyright Information: Bottom of the card
  - Artist Name: Bottom left corner
  - Edition Symbol: May appear on the left middle (1st Edition, Unlimited)
  - Card ID: Small print number below the artwork
  - Holographic Pattern: Various types depending on rarity (cosmos, reverse holo, etc.)

### Energy Cards
These provide the power for Pokémon to use their moves:
- Basic Energy cards (matching the Pokémon types)
- Special Energy cards with unique effects
- Can typically only attach one energy card per turn
- No HP or other combat stats
- Usually have a simple design featuring the energy symbol

### Trainer Cards
These represent items, tools, supporters, and stadiums that affect gameplay. They break down into:

#### Items
- Can play multiple per turn (unless specified)
- One-time use effects
- Instantly resolved when played

#### Tools
- Special type of Trainer card that attaches to Pokémon
- Only one tool can be attached to a Pokémon at a time
- Remains in play until the Pokémon is knocked out or the tool is removed
- Provides ongoing effects or boosts to the equipped Pokémon
- Can be identified by the "Tool" subtitle under "Trainer"
- Common effects include:
  - Boosting damage output
  - Adding resistance
  - Providing special abilities
  - Modifying retreat costs
  - Healing effects

#### Supporter Cards
- Limited to one per turn
- Usually feature a character artwork
- Typically provide stronger effects than items
- Must be discarded after use

#### Stadium Cards
- Only one can be active at a time
- Affects both players
- Remains in play until another stadium is played or removed
- Shows location artwork and ongoing effects
